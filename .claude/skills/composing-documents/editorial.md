# Editorial standards

Writing principles, tone, and style for producing any durable prose in this repo — notes, docs, README, skills, and any markdown written to file.

## Tone

Authoritative, declarative, terse. The voice of a contributor who has thought the design through — confident, precise, position-taking where a position is warranted, explicit about what is still open.

## Voice

- Second person ("you") — direct and professional.
- Present tense — "the method returns."
- Active voice — "the engine pushes memos," not "memos are pushed."
- State positions directly. Skip hedges (might, perhaps, could be, it's worth noting, might want to) and filler (basically, simply, just, actually, really, quite, very, in order to).
- Short sentences, short paragraphs — structure for skimming.

## Structure

- Open with a lede — one or two sentences naming what the doc is for.
- Self-contained pages — link to supporting concepts and include enough context for the page to stand alone.
- Sentence-case headings.
- Numbered steps for tasks; bullets for options.
- State each idea once.
- One brief example per rule is enough, and only when the rule is not self-evident.

## Formatting

- Prefer headings, bullets, numbered lists, or prose over tables.
- Positive assertions in principles, guidance, and instructions. Phrase rules as what to do.
- Write assertions, not commentary. Skip motivational framing and appeals to virtue; let each statement stand on its own.
- Em dash (—) for parenthetical breaks, definitions, and appositives. Use the em dash character, not double hyphens (--).
- Skip bold entirely. Headings, prose, bullets, and numbered lists carry the structure on their own.
- Oxford comma in lists of three or more — "nodes, edges, and memos."

## Example — converting a table to headings and bullets

Before:

```
| Name | Purpose |
|---|---|
| foo | does X |
| bar | does Y |
```

After:

```
### foo
Does X.

### bar
Does Y.
```
